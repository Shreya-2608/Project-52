Camel Origami

5 Fun Fact on Camels:

1.) Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.

2.) Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.

3.) Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.

4.) Camels can travel at up to 40 miles per hour – the same as a racehorse!

5.) Camels are very strong and can carry up to 900 pounds for 25 miles a day.

Teddy Bear Origami

5 Fun Facts on Teddy Bears:

1.) Teddy bears got their name when Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub.

2.) The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.

3.) Jackie Miley has the world’s biggest teddy bear collection, totaling 8,026 bear friends in 2012.

4.) The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff.

5.) The official term for a teddy bear collector is an ‘arctophile’.

Flying Cicada Origami

5 Fun Facts on Flying Cicadas

1.) Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies.

2.) Most have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes.

3.) Females may be attracted to the sound of motors.

4.) The loud whirring or buzzing sound you hear is an all-male cicada chorus.

5.) They improve lawns by digging tunnels that allow air into the soil. When they die, their rotting bodies put nitrogen into the soil.
